Utility computing is a process of acquisition, integration, mining, and analysis of big and heterogeneous data generated by a diversity of sources. It is highly related to computer science, economics, behavioural economics, sociology, psychology and others. In summary, utility computing is an new computing paradigm based on utility theory. Utility computing connects unobtrusive and ubiquitous fusion, indexing, mining and computing technologies, advanced data management and analytics models, and novel visualization methods, to improve the benefits of Data Science, Human Life, and Decision Making.

Utility computing is also a research project in UCMlab Research, led by Dr. Wensheng Gan since August 2018. By analyzing the big data generated with rich information, a series of utility computing applications have been enabled as follows. One of core research problems is to fuse rich data across different domains. The other is to learn useful knowledge from different types of data, e.g. transaction data, sequential data, complex events, sensor data in WSNs, data from IoTs, and spatio-temporal data. Several survey papers on utility computing & mining can be referred as follows:

[1] Wensheng Gan et al. A Survey of Utility-Oriented Pattern Mining. IEEE TKDE 2019, or arXiv 2018. (A Tutorial)

[2] Wensheng Gan et al. A survey of incremental high‐utility itemset mining. Wiley DMKD, 2018. (A Tutorial)
